Discussion points

  • editing rego after payment (is_paid vs $0 balance vs discount codes)
  • non sequential id numbers, possibly used as capabilities (eg. public unique URL for invoices)
    • as capabilities they wouldn't be very good if they don't expire
    • perhas some other mechanism for letting somebody else pay for an invoice
  • invoice uniqueness and history and holding places until due date, extending due date, ensure regeneration keeps due date unless intentionally changed by organiser. LOTS OF ISSUES with this.
  • multiple fields for some data (eg. phone number, nickname, ????)
  • miniconf organiser role vs list
    • the hard-coded list was a mistake on my (Jiri's) part, it needs to go away in favour of either a role (maybe the existing mini-conf role), or automatically detecting people who are associated with an accepted mini-conf proposal (like speakers are detected).
  • is_speaker vs Speaker
    • failed experiment, needs to be unified
  • Volunteer vs volunteer
    • Volunteer is a $0 ticket type
    • volunteer is (Volunteer) or (has indicated an area where they're willing to volunteer, part of the time)
  • program scheduling (GUI editor, mini-conf organisers having access, producing public pages) possibly including attendee schedules, sms/email notifications, and any other bling.
  • meaning of the word "registration" (make it consistent, and unambiguous). Currently, it means about four different things:
    • has an account on the website ("signed up")
    • filled in details but not yet paid ("tentatively registered")
    • paid, or otherwise entitled to attend ("registered and paid")
    • picked up badge at rego desk ("checked in")
  • accommodation, how much of a hack?
  • additional fields on rego form:
    • request planet info from attendees? (specify lca only feed)
    • request gpg fingerprint from attendees (possibly whole key),
      • possibly print some small fingerprints with details for badge
  • badge generation method?
    • 2007 had coloured parts of the badge pre-printed (only 2 kinds), then printed the delegate info in b&w using a search-and-replace on the SVG file. [AFAIK - any of the 2007 people, correct me, please?]
    • 2008 exported the data (using /admin/badge_data) and had it professionally printed and put into holders and envelopes with name and rego number printed on envelope. Empty envelopes were used as record of attendance and entered into database. The envelopes were also used for notes about attendees who haven't yet checked in ("refer sol'n desk" or "notify [name] when he arrives"). Additional badges were printed on a colour printer in the noc.
  • enhance invoicing (eg. manual invoices)
    • 2008 manual invoices; the bulk of them would be eliminated by allowing changes after payment (accom for an existing paid rego, extending existing accom, additional t-shirts / dinner tickets / whatever for existing paid rego, upgrade from Hobbyist to Professional) and group registration. However, even then there would be a few manual ones:
      • adjustment of invoice for Fairy Penguin sponsors with part cash, part in-kind sponsorship
      • charging for use of equipment to open day tables (TV rental, projector rental)
      • rego invoices issued after close of conf (or have some other mechanism to allow this)
  • lots and lots and lots of caching
  • drop down to select relevant miniconf for rejected proposals
    • I think that exists...
  • multiple speaker proposals
    • really needs to be done!
    • make it clear that people who aren't co-speakers at paper selection time can't be added later
  • the extra t-shirt order should have proper sizes/styles selection rather than a free-form text field

  • Admin - Would be good if profile list showed roles. Eg.
    • A. user
    • B. registered to attend
    • C. registered AND paid.
    • D. submitted paper / mini conf
    • E. accepted speaker
    • F. volunteer
    • G. organiser
    • H. reviewer
  • Do "current temp in melbourne" directly rather than via PHP. - There is an XML feed with th info, the PHP script parses TXT.
  • "timetable planner" in which attendees can plan the talks which they want to go to. This would also be useful for figuring out numbers.
  • support multi-author papers
  • automatically start all the sequences at different points, so as to avoid confusion between person ID, account ID, invoice ID and rego ID. Workaround: 
SELECT setval('invoice_id_seq', 30000);
SELECT setval('registration_id_seq', 20000);
SELECT setval('account_id_seq', 40000);
SELECT setval('person_id_seq', 50000);
SELECT setval('rego_note_id_seq', 70000);
  • the extra t-shirt order should have proper sizes/styles selection rather than a free-form text field
  • Keep track of registrations and payments better, so that the system automagically copes with changing amount owed, getting people to pay just the additional amounts for bits they've added (or bits that were added into the database for them). This would probably mean allowing multiple registrations per person; latest one with cost<=total_paid is the one that counts (except for calculation of amount owed). That way, the whole registration can be edited.
    • Also handle refunds.
    • Alternately, payments could behave like discount codes - holding a place under the conf ceiling (and accom limit), but otherwise not blocking anything. On subsequent invoices, they would show up as "Payment - thank you". When invoice is paid or regenerated with balance <= zero, the type of rego and accom being held is updated.
    • A third possibility would be to have a mechanism for accom (and additional nights of existing accom); that seems to be the most common use-case, when someone pays their rego and later realises that they'll need accom (or additional accom).
  • Invoices should have proper way of being marked invalid (currently done by $0.01 items, no more than four) as well as "manual - not to be regenerated" (which might also e-mail the cttee when paid). Once there's a status code, it might as well also have the options "paid" and "zero", to save having to re-calculate these all the time. A "status reason" field would probably look better on the invoice than additional items, though I guess additional items work well enough.
  • Some way for organisers to mark "hold rego & accom" for people who have payment problems just before these sell out. For rego, this can be done with 0% discount codes, but accom cannot...
  • earlybirds taken should only be counted in one place (currently: controllers/registration.py and templates/registration/list.myt)

  •  ? condorcet/beatpath voting for best-ofs with web 2.0 drag-n-drop thingy?
